Overview

According to a survey conducted by HubSpot, of digital marketing experts emphasize that understanding audience interest is crucial for product development and planning marketing efforts to create personalized customer experience. This insight allows businesses to tailor their strategies to meet the specific needs and preferences of their target audience, leading to more effective marketing campaigns and successful product launches.

Source data

Google Trends provides valuable data of the popularity of search queries over time, which can be a powerful tool for understanding market trends and consumer behavior.However, raw data alone is not very useful; it needs to be analyzed to extract meaningful insights and identify patterns or trends. Analysis involves piecing together various data points to form a comprehensive understanding of the overall picture.

TOP queries

The most popular search queries. Scoring is on a relative scale where a value of 100 is the most commonly searched query, 50 is a query searched half as often as the most popular query, and so on.

Rising queries

Tueries with the biggest increase in search frequency since the last time period. Results marked 'Breakout' had a tremendous increase, probably because these queries are new and had few (if any) prior searches.

Breakout requests

The Rising tab represents terms that were searched for with the term you entered and had the most significant growth in volume over the selected time period. You’ll see a percentage of the Rising term’s growth compared to the previous time period. If you see “Breakout” instead of a percentage, it means that the search term grew by more than 5000%.

The search queries can be categorized into several key themes, reflecting the interests within the Netherlands related to digital marketing, business, and data analytics:

  1. Core Digital Marketing Disciplines:
  • Search Engine Optimization (SEO): A strong interest in all aspects of SEO, from basic optimization to advanced topics like SEO audits, local SEO (geo vs seo), and technical SEO (SEO score checkers, extensions). The Arabic query "سئو سیگنال" (SEO signal) hints at an interest in understanding SEO signals and algorithm updates.
  • Paid Advertising (Google Ads): Focus on Google Ads, covering keyword research, campaign setup, cost calculation, and the Display Network. This indicates a practical interest in running and managing Google Ads campaigns.
  • Social Media Marketing: Interest in social media is present but less defined than SEO and Google Ads. Queries around "DM social media" suggest interest in direct messaging and potentially social media customer service or direct marketing. The unusual queries related to "trump gaza" are likely trending news searches and less directly related to marketing strategy, but could indirectly signal an interest in current events and how social media is used for information dissemination.
  • Affiliate Marketing: A clear interest in affiliate marketing, particularly "high ticket affiliate marketing," and specific programs like "Temu affiliate program" and "Lego affiliate marketing." This indicates a desire to explore revenue generation through affiliate partnerships.
  • Internet Marketing/Digital Marketing (General): Broader queries around "online marketing specialist," "digital marketing experts," and "basisboek online marketing" (basic book on online marketing) suggest a need for foundational knowledge and career information in digital marketing.
  1. Website & E-commerce Focus:
  • Shopify & E-commerce Platforms: "Shopify" is prominently featured, pointing to strong interest in e-commerce platforms. Queries around "vidaxl dropshipping" and "auto ds" indicate interest in dropshipping business models.
  • Website Performance (Analytics): Although not explicitly stated as "website analytics," the strong presence of "Google Analytics" and "Google Tag Manager" queries, along with "steam analytics" and "slack analytics," shows interest in measuring and analyzing online platform performance.
  1. Business & Revenue Generation:
  • Revenue & Business Models: Queries about "omzet" (revenue in Dutch), "omzet bedrijf opzoeken" (find company revenue), and "business model canvas" show a clear focus on business viability and understanding revenue streams. The inclusion of "just eat takeaway" and stock-related searches ("nike stock," "lemonade stock") might reflect an interest in business performance of well-known companies.
  1. Data Analysis & Analyst Roles:
  • Data Analytics & Data Analysis: Strong interest in data analytics, data analysis techniques, and the role of a "data analist" (data analyst). Queries about "udemy" and "coursera" in this context point to individuals seeking to learn data analysis skills.
  • Analytics Tools (Google Analytics, Slack Analytics, Steam Analytics): Practical interest in using analytics tools across different platforms (web, communication, gaming).
  1. Strategy & Communication:
  • Marketing Strategy: Queries on "digital marketing strategies," "strategic alignment model," and "git branching strategy" (though technical, it reflects strategic thinking) indicate an interest in planning and strategic approaches in marketing and business.
  • Communication Skills: The inclusion of "roos van leary" (Leary's Rose) suggests an interest in communication models and interpersonal dynamics, relevant for marketing, customer interaction, and team collaboration.
  1. Dutch Market Context (Implied & Explicit):
  • Dutch Language Queries: "omzet bedrijf opzoeken," "wat doet een data analist," "aantal toeschouwers arena," "nos digitaal geld," "onlinemarketing amersfoort," "basisboek online marketing," "google analytics certificaat" in Dutch all strongly suggest the target audience is within the Netherlands or Dutch-speaking.
  • Dutch Company/Brand Mentions: "just eat takeaway" (Dutch origin), "vidaxl" (Dutch company), "onlinemarketing amersfoort" (Amersfoort is a Dutch city), "nos digitaal geld" (NOS is Dutch public broadcasting).
  • Geo vs SEO: Indicates interest in local SEO, which is crucial for businesses targeting the Dutch market geographically.
